HOW RESPONSIBLE 100 WORKS
"Responsible 100 has developed a methodology for practicing responsibility as a skill. They're enabling and supporting companies to very consciously and actively be part of something better. They enable businesses to balance their commercial objectives with the needs of people and planet. And to work hard at it, get better at it, and reap due reward, rather than just hoping or expecting everything will somehow sort itself out."
Spokesperson, Extinction Rebellion

Your Responsible 100 array is a constantly evolving record and account of your responsibility priorities, performance, risks and opportunities.

We support businesses of all shapes, sizes, maturities and locations to undertake the following steps…

STEP #1

Frist, consider the social, environmental and ethical issues that affect your company. We currently explore 58 separate responsibility issues, across up to 8 categories. Working out how prioritise them is where we start. This can be done by one responsible person within a company, or better by several people, and ideally with everyone in the business invited to get involved and express their views as to which responsibility issues are important, and which are not, for the company to be abreast of. 

STEP #2

Your first array will be an ordered responsibility issues heat map, and it will created in no time at all. The things to concentrating on most are presented on the left hand side, the things to free your mind of (at least for now) will be on the right, and those issues of moderate relevance or materiality in between.

STEP #3

Next we’ll work out how you currently perform on your priority issues. We do this by prompting some basic disclosures as to relevant practices or policies - possibly involving a selection of employees or other stakeholders if that suits you - and asking a few follow up questions. We’ll quickly build up a picture and basic written summary of your current policies and practices. These written accounts may form the basis for internal or external reporting in due course.

STEP #4

Now we can answer that most critical of questions: where should our business invest its precious resources to affect the most urgently needed change? In the below example, that would be on Reducing Social Exclusion and Corporate Culture, two top priority (or 'must report on') issues, where in the example, performance is currently POOR.
